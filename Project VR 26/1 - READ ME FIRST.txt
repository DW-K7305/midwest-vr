                  MidWest-VR — Quick Start
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

You don't need to know any code. After one-time Setup,
launching the app is just a double-click.

Your SSD is named "SandSpd-EX" and the project lives at:

    /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/

⚠️  IMPORTANT — your work Mac may have Terminal blocked.
That's fine. The Setup step needs Terminal, but the App
itself does NOT. So Setup happens once on a DIFFERENT Mac,
then you carry the SSD to your work Mac and just double-click.

Read which path applies to you below, then follow only that
path.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHICH PATH IS MINE?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Try this on your work Mac: Press Cmd+Space, type "Terminal",
press Return.

  ✅ Terminal opens normally → Path A (one Mac).
  🚫 Terminal blocked, won't open, locked by IT → Path B (two Macs).

If unsure, assume Path B. It's safer.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                       PATH A — ONE MAC
              (Terminal is allowed on this Mac)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STEP 1 — Confirm the project folder is on the SSD at:
             /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/

         Open Finder, press Cmd+Shift+G, paste that path,
         and press Return. You should see files like
         "1 - READ ME FIRST.txt" and "2 - Start Here.command".

STEP 2 — In that folder, double-click "2 - Start Here.command".

         If macOS warns "unidentified developer":
           Right-click the file → Open → Open

         When the script asks, type YES and press Return.
         Wait ~10 minutes while it compiles.

STEP 3 — When you see "✅  Setup complete", close Terminal.
         "MidWest-VR.app" is now in
         /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/.
         Double-click MidWest-VR.app.

That's it. Future launches: just double-click MidWest-VR.app.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                      PATH B — TWO MACS
        (Terminal is blocked on your work computer)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This path NEVER touches your work Mac with anything that
needs admin or Terminal. The work Mac only ever runs the
finished App from the SSD by double-click.

You'll need temporary access to ANY Mac with Terminal — your
home Mac, a friend's Mac, a Mac at an Apple Store/library, an
older personal Mac in a closet. It can be Intel or Apple
Silicon, macOS 13 or newer. You only need it for ~10 minutes,
once. Apple Silicon (M-series) is preferred so the compiled
app runs at full speed on your work Mac (which is M4).

──── DO THIS ON THE OTHER MAC, ONCE ──────────────────────────

STEP B1 — Plug the SandSpd-EX SSD into the other Mac.

STEP B2 — Open Finder, press Cmd+Shift+G, paste this path
          and press Return:
              /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/

STEP B3 — Double-click "2 - Start Here.command".

          If macOS warns "unidentified developer":
            Right-click the file → Open → Open

          When the script asks, type YES and press Return.
          Wait ~10 minutes for the colored output to finish.
          When you see "✅  Setup complete", close Terminal.
          MidWest-VR.app is now in
          /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/.

STEP B4 — Eject the SSD from the other Mac (right-click drive
          in Finder → Eject). Unplug it.

──── DO THIS ON YOUR WORK MAC ────────────────────────────────

STEP B5 — Plug the SSD into your work Mac.

STEP B6 — Open Finder, press Cmd+Shift+G, paste:
              /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/

          Double-click "MidWest-VR.app".

          If macOS warns "unidentified developer":
            Right-click MidWest-VR.app → Open → Open

That's it. Future days: just plug in the SSD and double-click
MidWest-VR.app. No Terminal involved on the work Mac, ever.

SAFETY ON A WORK MAC — READ THIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

What runs at runtime on your work Mac:

  ▸ MidWest-VR.app — a normal Mac application living on the
    SSD at /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/MidWest-VR.app.
    Launches in your user account. No admin password.
  ▸ A bundled "adb" inside the app, used to talk to Quest 2
    headsets over USB. Standard Android tool.
  ▸ Reads/writes one config file: MidWest-VR.config.json
    (next to the app, on the SSD).

What does NOT happen on your work Mac:

  ✗ No system-wide install.
  ✗ No admin password prompt.
  ✗ No Homebrew, no Xcode tools, no Rust, no Node.
  ✗ No network calls from the app itself.
  ✗ No iCloud, no keychain, no files outside the SSD folder.
  ✗ No background process or login item.

Things you SHOULD check with your IT department first:

  • Is connecting USB peripherals (specifically Meta Quest 2
    headsets in Developer Mode) allowed on your work Mac?
    Some MDM-managed Macs block USB Accessories.
  • Is running a non-App-Store, ad-hoc-signed app from a
    USB drive allowed by your acceptable-use policy?
  • Some EDR (security) tools log adb processes. That's
    expected and normal — adb is a widely used Android tool.

For the deeper details, read docs/SAFETY.md.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THINGS YOU MIGHT WONDER ABOUT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

▸ "I don't have access to a second Mac. Now what?"
  Options, easiest first:
   1. Borrow one for 10 minutes (a friend, a relative, a
      neighbor — any Mac with macOS 13+).
   2. Apple Store / public library Macs sometimes work.
   3. Ask your IT to do the Setup step on a sandboxed Mac.
   4. Cloud Mac services (MacInCloud, MacStadium) — overkill
      but real options.

▸ "I can't see MidWest-VR.app in
   /Volumes/SandSpd-EX/MidWest-VR/."
  Setup hasn't run successfully yet. Re-run the Setup step
  on the OTHER Mac (Path B Step B3).

▸ "Setup says some download failed."
  Likely a school firewall blocking GitHub or Google. Try a
  home network for the one-time Setup.

▸ "Where does MidWest-VR save its settings?"
  In MidWest-VR.config.json next to MidWest-VR.app on the
  SSD. Plug the SSD into a different Mac, your settings
  come with you. (Details: docs/PORTABLE_SSD.md.)

▸ "My SSD shows up as a different name in Finder."
  If the SSD isn't actually named "SandSpd-EX" the path will
  be different — replace SandSpd-EX with whatever name shows
  up under /Volumes/ on your Mac. Run this on a Mac with
  Terminal: `ls /Volumes/` to list every connected drive.

▸ "Will running the app leave a mess on my work Mac?"
  No. Nothing is installed. Unplug the SSD and the work Mac
  has zero trace of MidWest-VR. (Read docs/SAFETY.md.)

▸ "I want to update the app later."
  Re-run the Setup step on your other Mac. Or just keep
  using the build you have — there's no auto-update.
